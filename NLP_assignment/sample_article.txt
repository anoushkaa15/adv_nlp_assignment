City officials announced a new climate resilience plan on Tuesday after weeks of heavy flooding damaged several neighborhoods. The mayor said the plan will expand drainage systems, restore wetlands, and create emergency cooling centers before next summer. Supporters described the proposal as a long-overdue investment in public safety, while critics argued that the announcement was rushed and did not include enough detail about funding. The article quoted residents who said they were frustrated by repeated flood warnings and slow repairs. It also cited a municipal budget summary showing that the first phase would cost about $42 million over three years.
